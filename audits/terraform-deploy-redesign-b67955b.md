# Terraform deployment + IAM + CI redesign review — executive report

Audited tree: `origin/main @ b67955b` (2026-07-21). Structured companion:
`audits/terraform-deploy-redesign-b67955b.yaml` (13 findings, 10 rejected candidates, full
question answers, rubric, and per-component disposition). This report is the human-first layer.

## Why deploys keep needing admin (Q1)

Not the reason the system's own documentation would suggest. The guard, the gated-apply
Environment, the convergence latch — the visible machinery — are all working as designed. The
stall lives one layer down: **three nested allow-lists must all admit a change, and only the
first is visible at plan time.**

1. **The deployer role's enumerated write surface (primary).** `github_ci_apply`'s inline policy
   grants writes per service, per ARN: three named alarms, five named EventBridge rules, no
   `logs:CreateLogGroup`, no `lambda:CreateFunction`/`UpdateFunctionConfiguration`/`TagResource`.
   Any change introducing a new resource — or touching an existing one in an unenumerated way —
   passes the guard (it isn't IAM) and then dies AccessDenied at apply, latching the record red.
   One gated apply (#644) accumulated **six** AccessDenieds (rec-2754); four are still missing
   (rec-2757); a config-only PR on the prod Lambdas would fail today (rec-2703). Decision 129
   diagnosed this exact class on the *read* axis — "a new resource always outruns the enumerated
   list by one PR/apply cycle — recurred 15+ times in ~6 weeks" — and fixed reads with
   `agent-platform-*` prefixes plus a coverage verifier. Writes were left enumerated.
2. **The IAM-write budget stops at branch/pr, and new roles are declared boundary-less.** So the
   gated-apply path is *structurally unable to execute* most of what the guard routes to it: the
   human approves, the apply fails `iam:CreateRole`/`iam:PutRolePolicy`, and the work falls to
   the admin tier anyway (observed live at T2.24; ratified as the intended model by Decision 98).
   The approval click is spent on an act the executor cannot perform.
3. **Phantom layer replaces.** Non-content-addressed rebuilt zips produce spurious
   "must be replaced" layer diffs that route to gated-apply "on every recurrence" (#646's own
   words). T2.42 owns this; it is unbuilt.
4. **Recovery is a chain, not a button.** After an admin fixes an episode out-of-band, the saved
   plan is stale, Reconcile fails closed on it, and the operator must know to run the dispatch-ack
   re-plan instead.

**The empirical sizing:** of the 12 most recent commits touching `terraform/personal` or the
apply workflows, 10 carried infra diffs; only 3 completed autonomously (all trivial), while 7
required a human beyond the merge — approval clicks that then failed, admin `-target` applies, an
out-of-band `aws iam put-role-policy` later codified as a direct-to-main commit, and a
bootstrap-root grant. **~70% of recent infra changes could not complete through the autonomous
path, and over half of the sample was itself IAM-grant repair generated by the enumerated model.**
The treadmill feeds its own change stream. Notably, the T2.25 in-budget lane was used by *zero*
sampled plans — shared policy fragments couple in-budget roles (branch/pr) to out-of-budget ones
(plan/drift), so any shared change routes the whole plan.

Verdict: `multiple-compounding`, dominated by the allow-list family — with the crucial nuance
that the binding allow-list is the **AWS-side identity policy, not the guard budget**. The budget
is a deliberate mirror of `IAMRoleWriteBounded`; widening the JSON without widening the grant
changes nothing.

## The requester's allow-list diagnosis (Q2): confirmed — and the deny-list is half-built

The boundary policy (`agent-platform-github-ci-apply-boundary`) is already a genuine deny-list
artifact: broad data-plane allows plus `DenyIAMEscalation` (boundary-propagation condition),
`DenyBoundaryRemoval`, `DenyBoundaryPolicyModification` — precisely the spec's SCP substitute,
built and verified. What's missing is the inversion: today a narrow allow-list identity policy
sits *inside* a deny-capable ceiling, giving the worst of both (perpetual grant-chasing, none of
the structural freedom). An SCP requires Organizations; this boundary-plus-condition pattern is
the correct single-account substitute and is already proven here.

## The redesign's shape (Q7): invert, don't rebuild

The control theory — saved-plan no-TOCTOU, fail-closed guard, server-side red latch, Environment
gate, bootstrap fixed point — is the repo's best work and survives intact (CH8 resolved:
targeted repair beats replacement). The change is one inversion plus fences:

- **Phase 0 (days):** validate non-empty gated-apply reviewers (rec-2307); delete the stale
  unguarded `deploy.yml`; **deny PlatformDev the convergence/tfplan/tfstate prefixes** (DEP-13 —
  today the ambient runtime identity can spoof the anti-masking anchor and, via ExternalIds
  readable in tfstate, escalate DEV-container key theft to PlatformAdmin `iam:*`); verify the
  saved plan's sha256 against the PR-reviewed artifact at fetch (DEP-07).
- **Phase 1 (planned already):** execute T2.42 — content-addressed zips + layer replace policy
  (kills the top gated-apply generator), committed lockfile — and consolidate the four copies of
  the apply/record/build chain into one reusable workflow (DEP-08, rec-2663).
- **Phase 2 (the inversion — amends Decisions 98 and 92 pt 5, explicitly flagged):** one
  admin apply that attaches the existing boundary to **all** `agent-platform-*` roles (today
  3 of 17), widens `IAMRoleWriteBounded`/`CreateBounded` to `role/agent-platform-*` (keeping the
  boundary condition, self-exclusion, and all self-protection denies), broadens the apply role's
  data-plane **writes** to the same per-service prefix pattern Decision 129 ratified for reads,
  and widens the budget table to match. End state: routine IAM auto-applies in-budget; a new role
  ships through **one gated approval that actually works**; the admin tier shrinks to
  bootstrap/boundary amendments — the spec's fixed point. Add resource-based-policy types to the
  guard (DEP-06) *no later than this phase*, since write-broadening removes the accidental
  identity-policy backstop.
- **Phase 3:** a path-scoped review requirement (CODEOWNERS-style, admin-bypassable, non-wedging)
  over the ~6 gate files — guard, budget JSON, apply workflows, bootstrap/github modules. Today
  they merge with zero human review and a workflow self-edit reaches the apply role without any
  Environment (the CD.35-acknowledged residual, closed on the AWS side only).
- **Phase 4 (conditional, at Decision 77's own `live_full` trigger):** an Organizations shell
  with an org CloudTrail — the one property single-account cannot fake. **Multi-account now is
  not worth it** for a solo dev: its blast-radius benefit is approximated by the boundary +
  bootstrap tier once DEP-05/DEP-13 close, at a fraction of the carry cost. Decision 77 is
  retained, not amended.

## Highest-leverage single change

**DEP-02 (with DEP-01, the same admin apply): make the gated path executable.** Boundary on all
roles + `role/agent-platform-*` write budget + prefix-scoped data-plane writes converts
gated-apply from approve-then-AccessDenied ceremony into the working end-to-end channel the
design always intended, and retires the grant-lag treadmill that generates most of the change
stream. Everything else is polish by comparison.

## Honest maturity call

Per-surface, computed under the pinned thresholds: **apply-model: frontier** (the contract layer
is genuinely excellent — single SoT, machine-readable routing, honest scars);
**workflow-fleet: frontier** by the letter of the 0-critical/0-high rule, though it carries real
consolidation debt (two mediums); **guard: strong** (one shared high — the unprotected amendment
channel — plus the resource-policy blind spot); **apply-pipeline: nascent** and
**iam-identities: nascent** — each carries two-to-three highs (enumerated write surface,
non-executable gated path, PlatformDev's reach into the anchor and state, the unprotected gate
files). The nascent ratings deserve their sting, but note their character: the pipeline's own
control *logic* is near-frontier; the highs all live in the **authority model around it** —
grants, boundaries, and repo-side protection — which is exactly the layer the Phase 0–3 plan
rewrites with parts already on the shelf. Checklist roll-up (Q8): 4 met, 8 partial, 0 missed —
**adequate**, one inversion away from strong.

Fraction that matters, restated once: ~70% of recent real infra changes needed a human; the
target the design itself ratified is "near-zero gate frequency." The gap between those two
numbers is this audit, and it closes with roughly one week of sequenced work, one deliberate
Decision amendment (98 / 92 pt 5), and zero new architecture.
